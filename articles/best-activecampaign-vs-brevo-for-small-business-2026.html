ActiveCampaign vs Brevo: The Complete Small Business Comparison

Choosing between ActiveCampaign and Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) can make or break your email marketing strategy. Both platforms serve small businesses but take dramatically different approaches to pricing, features, and complexity.

After testing both platforms extensively with real small business campaigns, I'll break down exactly which tool fits different business scenarios, budgets, and growth plans.

Email Marketing Capabilities

ActiveCampaign's Strengths

ActiveCampaign excels in sophisticated email sequences. The automation builder lets you create complex customer journeys based on behavior, tags, and custom fields. You can trigger emails based on:

  • Website visits to specific pages
  • Purchase history and amounts
  • Email engagement patterns
  • Custom event tracking

The segmentation capabilities are genuinely impressive. I've set up campaigns targeting "customers who bought Product A but haven't opened emails in 30 days" – that level of granularity drives real results.

Brevo's Approach

Brevo keeps automation simpler but still effective. The workflow builder covers essential scenarios like welcome sequences, abandoned cart recovery, and birthday campaigns. While less sophisticated than ActiveCampaign, it handles 80% of what most small businesses need.

The transactional email features are actually superior to ActiveCampaign, making Brevo excellent for e-commerce businesses needing order confirmations, shipping updates, and password resets.

CRM and Sales Features

This is where the platforms diverge most dramatically. ActiveCampaign includes a full CRM with:

  • Deal pipeline management
  • Lead scoring based on behavior
  • Sales automation sequences
  • Task management for follow-ups

Brevo's CRM is more basic contact management. You can store customer information and interaction history, but there's no true sales pipeline or advanced lead scoring.

For service businesses or B2B companies, ActiveCampaign's CRM integration is invaluable. You can see a prospect's entire journey from first website visit to closed deal.

Deliverability and Performance

Email deliverability can make or break campaigns. In testing with identical lists:

ActiveCampaign consistently achieved 96-98% deliverability across Gmail, Outlook, and Yahoo. The platform's reputation management and authentication tools are enterprise-grade. Brevo averaged 94-96% deliverability – still excellent but slightly behind ActiveCampaign. The difference becomes meaningful with larger campaigns. Both platforms include:
  • DKIM and SPF authentication
  • Dedicated IP options (paid plans)
  • Spam testing and content analysis
  • Unsubscribe management

User Experience and Learning Curve

ActiveCampaign

The interface is powerful but overwhelming initially. New users often struggle with:

  • Understanding automation logic
  • Setting up proper tagging systems
  • Navigating between email and CRM features

However, once mastered, the efficiency gains are substantial.

Brevo

Much more intuitive for beginners. The dashboard is cleaner, and core functions are easier to locate. You can launch effective campaigns within hours of signing up.

The tradeoff: you'll eventually hit feature limitations as your marketing sophistication grows.

Real-World Use Cases

Choose ActiveCampaign If:

  • You're a service business with complex sales cycles
  • Customer lifetime value justifies higher tool costs
  • You want advanced behavioral tracking and segmentation
  • Your team can invest time in learning the platform
  • You send frequent, highly personalized campaigns

Choose Brevo If:

  • You're just starting with email marketing
  • Budget is a primary concern
  • You need SMS marketing included
  • Your campaigns are relatively straightforward
  • You prioritize ease of use over advanced features

Choose HubSpot If:

  • You want a complete marketing suite
  • Content marketing is crucial to your strategy
  • You can eventually invest in paid features
  • You prefer one platform for all marketing activities

Migration and Setup

Moving to ActiveCampaign requires planning:
  • Export contacts with tags and custom fields
  • Recreate automation workflows
  • Set up integrations and tracking
  • Train team on new interface
Switching to Brevo is simpler:
  • Basic contact import process
  • Simpler automation recreation
  • Faster team adoption
  • Less complex setup requirements

Both platforms offer migration assistance, but ActiveCampaign's is more comprehensive due to feature complexity.

Bottom Line: Which Should You Choose?

For most growing small businesses, ActiveCampaign is worth the investment. The superior automation, CRM integration, and deliverability create measurable ROI improvements that justify higher costs.

Choose ActiveCampaign if you're serious about email marketing as a growth channel and can invest in learning the platform properly.

Brevo excels for budget-conscious businesses and those wanting to test email marketing without commitment. The free plan is genuinely useful, and paid plans offer solid value.

Choose Brevo if you're starting out, have budget constraints, or prefer simplicity over advanced features.

The hybrid approach: Many successful businesses start with Brevo to learn email marketing fundamentals, then migrate to ActiveCampaign as they grow and need more sophisticated automation.

Both platforms will serve you well – the key is matching your choice to your current needs, budget, and growth trajectory. Start your free trial with whichever platform aligns better with your immediate priorities.
