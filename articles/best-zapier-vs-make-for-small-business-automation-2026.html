Zapier vs Make: Which Is Better for Small Businesses? (2026)

7 min read Mar 2026
TL;DR Zapier wins for beginners with its massive app library (7,000+ integrations) and intuitive interface, while Make excels for complex workflows with advanced logic and better pricing. For most small businesses just starting with automation, Zapier's ease of use makes it the better choice despite higher costs.

Why Small Businesses Need Workflow Automation

Small business owners wear countless hats—sales, marketing, customer service, accounting. Without automation, you're manually copying data between apps, sending repetitive emails, and losing hours on tasks a computer could handle in seconds.

Workflow automation tools like Zapier and Make connect your business apps, automatically triggering actions when specific events occur. When a new lead fills out your contact form, automation can instantly add them to your CRM, send a welcome email, and notify your sales team—all without manual intervention.

The question isn't whether you need automation (you do), but which platform will serve your small business best. Let's break down the two leading contenders.

Zapier vs Make: The Complete Breakdown

App Integrations: Zapier's Massive Advantage

Zapier dominates with over 7,000 app integrations, including virtually every business tool you're likely using. Whether you need to connect ClickUp for project management, ActiveCampaign for email marketing, or FreshBooks for invoicing, Zapier has you covered.

Make offers around 1,900+ integrations—still substantial, but significantly fewer. However, Make's integrations tend to be more robust, offering deeper functionality and more trigger/action options per app.

Winner: Zapier (for breadth), Make (for depth)

Ease of Use: User Experience Matters

Zapier's interface feels like filling out a simple form. You select a trigger app, choose what event starts the automation, then pick actions for other apps to perform. The step-by-step wizard guides you through setup with plain English explanations.

Make uses a visual flowchart approach that looks more complex initially but offers incredible flexibility. You drag modules onto a canvas and connect them with lines, creating workflows that branch, loop, and make decisions based on data.

For non-technical users: Zapier wins hands down For users comfortable with visual workflows: Make provides more power

Pricing: Make's Clear Victory

Here's where Make significantly outshines Zapier:

Zapier Pricing:
  • Free: 100 tasks/month, 5 Zaps
  • Professional: $19.99/month (750 tasks)
  • Team: $49/month (2,000 tasks)
  • Company: $99/month (10,000 tasks)
Make Pricing:
  • Free: 1,000 operations/month
  • Core: $9/month (10,000 operations)
  • Pro: $16/month (10,000 operations + premium features)

Make provides 10x more monthly operations for half the price of Zapier's Professional plan. For small businesses running multiple automations, this difference is substantial.

Advanced Features and Logic

Make excels in complex automation scenarios. You can create workflows with multiple paths, conditional logic, loops, and data transformations. Need to process a CSV file row by row, apply different actions based on data values, and aggregate results? Make handles this easily.

Zapier recently added more advanced features like multi-step Zaps and path branching, but it still feels limited compared to Make's capabilities. Zapier's strength lies in simple, reliable automations rather than complex business logic.

Error Handling and Reliability

Both platforms handle errors gracefully, but with different approaches:

Zapier provides detailed error logs and automatic retry mechanisms. When something fails, you get clear explanations and suggested fixes. The platform rarely goes down and handles API rate limits well. Make offers more granular error handling options. You can define specific actions when errors occur, create fallback paths, and even ignore certain types of errors. However, troubleshooting can be more complex due to the visual nature of workflows.

Getting Started: Setup and Implementation

Zapier Setup (15 minutes)

  1. Sign up and connect your first app (e.g., ClickUp)
  2. Choose a trigger event (new task created)
  3. Connect the action app (ActiveCampaign)
  4. Configure the action (add contact to list)
  5. Test and activate

Make Setup (30-45 minutes)

  1. Create account and explore the visual editor
  2. Add trigger module and configure webhooks/app connections
  3. Design workflow logic with conditional paths
  4. Add filters and data transformation modules
  5. Test each step individually before full activation

Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them

Over-automation: Start simple with 2-3 key workflows before expanding Poor error handling: Always set up email notifications for failed automations Ignoring data limits: Monitor your monthly task/operation usage closely Complex workflows without documentation: Document your automations for team members

Bottom Line: Which Should You Choose?

Choose Zapier if: You're new to automation, need maximum app compatibility, and prefer simplicity over advanced features. It's the safer choice for most small businesses, especially those using popular SaaS tools like ClickUp, ActiveCampaign, and HubSpot. Choose Make if: You need complex workflows, want better value for money, and have some technical comfort. It's ideal for businesses with unique automation needs that require conditional logic and data transformation. For most small businesses, Zapier's ease of use and extensive integrations make it the better starting point. Once you've mastered basic automation and need more advanced features, Make becomes an attractive option.

The real winner? Using automation at all. Whether you choose Zapier or Make, automating even basic workflows will save hours weekly and reduce human error. Start with whichever platform feels more comfortable—you can always migrate or use both platforms for different use cases as your business grows.
